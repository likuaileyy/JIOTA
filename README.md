Trajectory association is a critical task in maritime sensor networks. Unknown rotational offsets between two radars cause severe cross-radar association errors, degrading cooperative detection performance. The conventional Coherent Point Drift Expectation-Maximization (CPD-EM) method relies solely on instantaneous spatial distributions, ignoring temporal continuity and failing to estimate the rotational parameters of both radars simultaneously. To overcome this, this paper proposes a trajectory-level joint iteration framework based on CPD-EM that jointly estimates dual-radar rotational offsets and cross-radar trajectory association. Cumulative soft association weights from CPD-EM naturally guide the offset estimation within an alternating iterative process. Experiments on a dual-radar simulation dataset derived from real Automatic Identification System (AIS) data demonstrate that, without any calibration targets, the proposed method achieves estimation errors of $0.053^\circ$ for $\alpha$ and $0.075^\circ$ for $\beta$, with a trajectory-level association precision (TrajP) of 99.50\%. These results significantly outperform the CPDEM-Seq baseline (95.68\%) and demonstrate the method's superior generalization and robustness to platform vibrations.
